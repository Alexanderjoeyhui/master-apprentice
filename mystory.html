I have a genuine appreciation
for craftsmanship, and for the
care that goes into making.
I love the hands-on making
of clothes and I wanted it to
be my life's work, so for the
longest time I had the dream of
becoming a Savile Row bespoke
tailor.

Unfortunately I couldn't pursue
this dream because I simply
didn't have the resources. I
couldn't afford to move to
London for the 4 to 6 years it
takes to complete the tailoring
apprenticeship with Savile Row
Bespoke.

Later I learned about loans for
start-ups and quickly realised
that starting a business was
the right path for me to take,
this meant that I could still do
the kind of work that I can be
proud of and earn a living doing
it. So after graduating from
University I spent the next 12
months planning everything.

Tailors and craftspeople
typically learn their skills by
becoming an apprentice and
are taught everything by a
master of their trade, but in my
position I had to teach myself,
I am my own master and I am
the apprentice.

Hence Master & Apprentice.

Alexander Joey Hui
Owner, Designer & Craftsman
